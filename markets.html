Market Spaces

Market spaces constituted recurring ephemeral spaces where merchants participated in commercial exchange under careful regulation of the king. Royal edicts regulated the types of goods available for sale at Les Halles and which merchants could gain access to the market. The invitation of foreign merchants to sell their goods at Les Halles rendered the market space a site of diplomacy, as these merchants gained permission from the king to sell their goods to French wholesalers.
